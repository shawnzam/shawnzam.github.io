I am the Director of Research Programming and Advanced Technologies at the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania. I lead a team of developers who support faculty research by designing and delivering advanced software solutions rooted in machine learning, artificial intelligence, and data engineering. For more than a decade, I've worked at the intersection of technology and academic research, helping bridge the gap between conceptual inquiry and technical execution.

In recent years, my focus has shifted toward applied generative AI within higher education. I've led the development of a classroom lecture transcription recall system powered by retrieval augmented generation and large language model APIs. This system enables students and instructors to interactively query and summarize lecture content using natural language. I've also driven projects that automate the collection and organization of unstructured information from across the internet, using generative AI APIs to transform raw, unstructured data into usable, structured research assets.

In 2024, I completed the Strategies for Accountable AI program through Wharton Executive Education, gaining insight from leaders in AI governance, ethics, bias mitigation, and regulatory frameworks.

I'm deeply interested in how generative AI can elevate research, productivity, and knowledge work across industries. At the same time, I remain mindful of the risks and unintended consequences of unvetted AI systems, as well as the overhyped promises that can cloud judgment in a fast-moving and often speculative technology marketplace.

In my spare time, I enjoy cooking, playing basketball, and spending time with my two kids. I also volunteer on the board of Germantown Life Enrichment Center, a community organization dedicated to enrichment and wellness in the Germantown neighborhood of Philadelphia.
